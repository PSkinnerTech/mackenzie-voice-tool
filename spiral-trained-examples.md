## 2. Families on the fence about joining Alpha School

Subject: If you are still deciding about Alpha

Hi [Parent First Name],

If you are still on the fence about Alpha, I do not see that as a problem.

I think it is a healthy way to choose a school.

Most parents who find Alpha are not casually shopping for something different. They are usually responding to something they can see in their child. Maybe school is too slow in one subject and too fast in another. Maybe their child is doing fine on paper but coming home flat. Maybe they are bright, curious, and slowly learning that school is something to endure.

Those are not small concerns.

Alpha is different from traditional school, and different should be questioned. You should ask how the academics work. You should ask how we know students are learning. You should ask what happens when a child struggles, what happens when a child races ahead, and how Guides actually know the kids in front of them.

Those are the questions I would ask too.

Here is the short version. We do not believe kids need to spend all day in passive instruction to get strong academic outcomes. We believe they need focused academic work at the right level and pace, and they need the rest of the day to build the life skills, confidence, friendships, and independence that make school feel worth showing up for.

Alpha is not the right fit for every family. I would rather say that plainly than try to sell you.

But if your child needs a school that takes motivation seriously, challenges them where they are, and gives them room to become more capable, it is worth seeing Alpha in person.

Come visit. Watch the kids work. Talk to the team. Ask the hard questions directly.

You can schedule a visit here: [link]

Or reply to this email with the concern you are still carrying. We will give you a straight answer.

MacKenzie

Hi [Parent First Name],

Summer should not feel like school with sunscreen.

Kids need room to move, play, build, make friends, try something new, and have days that feel different from the regular school year. At the same time, many parents are trying to solve a real problem: how do I keep my child growing without filling the summer with worksheets, tutoring, or hours of screen time that do not add up to much?

That is the problem the Alpha Summer Program is built around.

The goal is not to recreate the school year. The goal is to give kids a summer with structure, momentum, and a lot more life in it.

Students will spend part of the day working at the right level and pace so they keep academic momentum. Then they will move into hands-on projects, movement, challenges, friendships, and life skills work that feels like summer because kids are doing real things, not just being kept busy.
